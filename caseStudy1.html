Overview

E-Health Book: App

Project description

UX Case Study

Duration: 14th Febuary - 25th May

Product Overview

My case study delves into the designing and creation of an app that enables parents to review their children's health records, print the records to meet school or camp requirements, and see when their children’s healthcare visits are due. This app was designed to tackle issues encountered by parents, child's guardians and health workers.


Problem

Difficult to maintain health-book: Users struggled to maintain and take care of their child's health book.


Forget schedule to visit healthcare unit: Many users faced difficulty in remembering the schedule to visit their child's healthcare unit and it might hamper the children’s health.


Goal

Develop a mobile app that helps parents to maintain their child's health book and schedule to visit their child's healthcare unit.

Roles and Responsibilities

My Role

UI/UX Designer: Responsible for the overall design and user experience for E-health Book.

Team

My responsibility involves conducting user research, designing wireframes, developing the app's features, testing and iterating, and implementing the final design and functionality.

User Pain Points

1

Difficult to maintain mannual health book

Users expressed a need for digital health book to keep their children's health records.

This is the main reason why I chose to develop an app that helps parents to maintain their child's health book.

2

Emphasis on Flexibility and Convenience

Users prefer and prioritize digital health book over manual hand book as it would be more flexible and convenient to access across different environments and circumstances.

My design will focus on simple and flexible features that would be easy to use and understand by all the users.

3

Need for notification

Users needs to be notified when their child's healthcare visits are due.

My design will integrate intuitive features like notifications that would help users to know their schedule to visit healthcare unit and stay reminded of their child's healthcare visits.

4

Review and print

Users can review and print thier children's health records if needed.

My design will include a feature that allows users to review and print their child's health records. Retrieve and print the records to meet school or camp requirements.

Usability study: findings

In my usability studies for the digital health record app, I conducted two rounds of research to gather valuable insights from users. Round 1 revealed challenges with onboarding, navigation, and engagement, leading to iterative improvements implemented in Round 2. The findings from these studies informed targeted enhancements to optimize user experience and usability.

Round 1 findings

  • Users struggled with the initial onboarding process, requiring clearer instructions and simplified setup steps.
  • Navigation within the app was confusing for some users, leading to difficulty in finding print buttons.
  • Feedback highlighted the need for more interactive elements to enhance user engagement.

Round 2 findings

  • Improved onboarding based on user feedback resulted in higher completion rates and reduced user confusion.
  • Navigation enhancements, such as a redesigned menu structure and clearer labeling, improved user satisfaction and ease of use.
  • Introduction of features like notification to remind users about their healtcare visits schedule.
